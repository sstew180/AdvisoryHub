# 2026-27 Business Unit Plan
## City Infrastructure Operations — Transport and Roads Division
**Prepared by:** Sam Nguyen, Project Manager (acting Manager Operations)
**Endorsed by:** Director Infrastructure
**Date:** April 2026
**Planning period:** 1 July 2026 to 30 June 2027

---

## Division Overview

The Transport and Roads Division delivers maintenance, renewal, and minor capital works for the organisation's road, path, and drainage network. The division manages approximately 2,400 km of roads, 1,800 km of pathways and cycleways, and 850 drainage structures across the local government area.

**Staff:** 124 FTE (84 operational, 28 technical, 12 management/administrative)
**Annual budget:** $47.2 million (operating $31.4M, capital $15.8M)
**External contractors:** $18.3 million in contracted services annually

---

## Strategic Alignment

This plan delivers against the following Corporate Plan commitments:

- **Outcome 1.2:** Roads and pathways that meet the needs of our growing city
- **Outcome 3.1:** Infrastructure delivered efficiently and sustainably
- **Outcome 4.3:** A well-governed organisation that makes evidence-based decisions

The division's 2026-27 priorities have been shaped by:
- Growing maintenance backlog (estimated $12.4M, up from $9.8M in 2024-25)
- Increasing population in northern growth corridors (Coomera, Pimpama, Ormeau)
- QAO audit finding (2024-25) on asset data completeness (62% vs 78% sector average)
- Community satisfaction survey results (roads satisfaction: 58%, below 65% target)

---

## 2026-27 Priorities

### Priority 1 -- Reduce the Maintenance Backlog
The maintenance backlog has grown 27% over two years. This is the division's most significant challenge and the primary driver of declining community satisfaction.

**Target:** Reduce backlog from $12.4M to $10.0M by 30 June 2027 (19% reduction)
**Investment:** $4.2M additional maintenance allocation (above base program)
**Focus:** Northern growth corridors (highest complaint volume) and CBD pedestrian network (highest usage)

### Priority 2 -- Asset Data Completeness
The QAO finding on asset data completeness (62%) must be addressed. Incomplete data drives poor maintenance decisions and under/over-investment in renewal.

**Target:** Increase asset condition data completeness from 62% to 80% by 30 June 2027
**Investment:** $380,000 for condition assessment program (contracted survey)
**Focus:** Tier 1 and Tier 2 road assets (currently 58% data completeness)

### Priority 3 -- Capital Program Delivery
The 2025-26 capital program delivered 71% of budget (underspend of $4.6M), primarily due to contractor availability and design delays. This must improve.

**Target:** Deliver 90% of capital budget ($14.2M of $15.8M) by 30 June 2027
**Actions:** Earlier contractor engagement (Q1 rather than Q2), design completion by October 2026 for all projects, monthly capital delivery reporting to Director

### Priority 4 -- Safety Performance
Two lost-time injuries in 2025-26 (both involving manual handling). Target is zero LTIs.

**Target:** Zero lost-time injuries in 2026-27
**Investment:** $85,000 for manual handling training program and equipment upgrades
**Actions:** Manual handling risk assessment for all operational roles by August 2026

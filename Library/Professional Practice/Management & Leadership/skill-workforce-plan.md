SKILL: How to Prepare a Workforce Plan
VERSION: 1.0
DOMAIN: Professional Practice
LEVEL: Base
JURISDICTION: Queensland

PURPOSE
A workforce plan identifies the people capability a team, division, or organisation needs to deliver its strategy, assesses the gap between current and future capability, and outlines how that gap will be closed. It connects people strategy to organisational strategy. A workforce plan that is not connected to the operational plan and budget is aspirational. Its value is in driving concrete decisions about hiring, development, succession, and structure.

WHEN TO USE THIS SKILL
- User asks to "prepare a workforce plan" or "develop a people plan"
- User needs to assess whether the team has the capability to deliver the work program
- User is preparing for a budget cycle and needs to justify staffing levels
- User needs to plan for succession, capability gaps, or anticipated attrition
- User asks to "think through our people needs" for the next one to three years

STRUCTURE
Current workforce profile: Headcount, FTE, roles, classifications, tenure, age profile, and any known attrition risks.
Future demand: What capability and capacity will be needed to deliver the strategy and operational plan over the planning period.
Gap analysis: The difference between current supply and future demand. Quantified where possible.
Strategies to close the gap: Recruitment, development, succession, restructure, or outsourcing. Each strategy with a rationale.
Critical roles: Roles where a vacancy would have a significant impact on service delivery. Succession status for each.
Action plan: Specific actions, owners, and timelines for each strategy.
Budget implications: The cost of the workforce plan and the source of funding.
Risks: Key workforce risks -- attrition, skills scarcity, succession gaps -- and proposed mitigations.

KEY PRINCIPLES
- Build from demand, not supply. Start with what the organisation needs to deliver, then assess whether the current workforce can provide it. Do not start by counting heads and working backwards.
- Be honest about attrition risk. A workforce plan that does not acknowledge known retirement risks, flight risks, or structural vacancies will fail within 12 months.
- Critical roles require succession plans. A workforce plan that identifies critical roles without addressing succession is incomplete.
- Development takes time. A capability gap that will take 18 months to close through training cannot be resolved in time for a program that starts in six months. Plan the timing realistically.
- Cost the plan. A workforce plan without a budget is a wish list. Every strategy -- recruitment, development, succession -- has a cost that must be reflected in the budget.

QUALITY STANDARDS
A good workforce plan is one that a Director can use to make specific decisions about hiring, development, and structure. If the plan's action items are so general that they could apply to any organisation, they will not drive action. The gap analysis must be specific enough to identify which capabilities are short, by how many FTE, and by when the gap will become critical.

QUEENSLAND CONTEXT
Workforce plans for Queensland local government should align with the council's Corporate Plan people and capability commitments. The council's enterprise agreement specifies conditions relating to classification, flexible work, and workforce change that must be reflected in the plan. Workforce plans involving significant structural changes must follow the consultation requirements in the enterprise agreement and the Fair Work Act 2009. Plans that address workforce diversity should align with the council's equal employment opportunity and inclusion strategy. The Queensland Public Sector Commission provides useful workforce planning frameworks that are applicable to local government. Succession planning for Senior Contract Officer roles must comply with the council's executive employment framework.

EXAMPLE LANGUAGE
Critical role risk: The Manager Risk and Insurance position is currently held by an officer who has indicated an intention to retire within 18 months. This role is classified as critical -- a vacancy of more than 30 days would directly impair the council's ability to meet its LGR s207 obligations and could result in a qualified ARC report. Current succession depth: one internal candidate assessed as ready-now with development support; no other internal candidates within 12 months of readiness. Action: Commence a structured development program for the identified internal successor by 1 July 2026. If succession is not confirmed by December 2026, commence external recruitment process to run in parallel.
Gap analysis: To deliver the 2026-29 ICT strategy, the Technology Services division requires four additional roles with cloud architecture capability. Current supply: one officer with relevant skills. External market assessment indicates Queensland local government is competing with state government and private sector for a limited pool of cloud architects. Estimated time to fill: four to six months per role. Budget implication: four FTE at Band 7 average $140,000 total remuneration = $560,000 per annum recurrent from 2026-27.

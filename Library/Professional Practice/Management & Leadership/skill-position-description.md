SKILL: How to Write a Position Description
VERSION: 1.0
DOMAIN: Professional Practice
LEVEL: Base
JURISDICTION: Queensland

PURPOSE
A position description (PD) is a document that defines what a role does, the level at which it operates, the qualifications and experience required, and the key relationships involved. It is used for recruitment, classification, performance management, and organisational design. A good PD is accurate, current, and specific enough to attract the right candidates and set clear expectations without being so prescriptive that it becomes a job list.

WHEN TO USE THIS SKILL
- User asks to "write a position description" or "update a PD"
- User is creating a new role and needs to define it
- User is preparing for a recruitment process and needs to refresh the PD
- User needs to reclassify a position and must document the change in duties
- User asks to "define what this role does" in a formal document

STRUCTURE
Position details: Title, classification level, business unit, reports to, direct reports.
Position purpose: Two to three sentences. Why this role exists and what it contributes to the organisation.
Key accountabilities: The five to eight most important things this role is responsible for. Each accountability starts with a strong verb. Outcomes, not activities.
Key relationships: Internal and external stakeholders this role works with and the nature of those relationships.
Selection criteria: The qualifications, experience, knowledge, and skills required. Distinguish between essential (must have) and desirable (nice to have).
Special requirements: Any mandatory requirements -- licences, Working with Children Check, security clearance, physical requirements.
Classification basis: The organisational framework under which the role is classified (stream, level, salary band).

KEY PRINCIPLES
- Accountabilities are outcomes, not activities. "Manages the team" is an activity. "Leads a team of eight officers to deliver the council's risk management program within budget, achieving a minimum of 90% of annual plan commitments" is an accountability.
- Selection criteria must match the role. Do not include a postgraduate qualification as essential if the role does not genuinely require it -- it narrows the field unnecessarily and may create grounds for a discrimination claim.
- PDs must be current. A PD that does not reflect what the person in the role actually does creates classification disputes and performance management difficulties.
- Classification must be justified. The accountability statements are the evidence for the classification level. If the PD overstates responsibilities to achieve a higher classification, it creates industrial risk.
- Keep it to two pages. A PD that is too long will not be read by candidates and will not be maintained by managers.

QUALITY STANDARDS
A good PD is one that a candidate can read and know whether they want the job and whether they are qualified for it. It should also be the document a manager uses to set performance expectations on day one. If the role holder and their manager would describe the role differently, the PD is not doing its job.

QUEENSLAND CONTEXT
Position descriptions for Queensland local government employees must comply with the relevant enterprise agreement classification structure. The council's HR team maintains the approved PD template and classification framework. Reclassification of positions must follow the council's classification review process and may require union consultation under the enterprise agreement. Positions classified as Senior Contract Officers (executive employees under LGA s196) are governed by the council's senior contract officer framework. PDs for roles with significant financial delegation must identify the delegation level consistent with the council's delegation register. For positions involving access to personal information, the PD should reference privacy obligations under the Information Privacy Act 2009.

EXAMPLE LANGUAGE
Position purpose: The Manager Risk and Insurance leads the council's enterprise risk management and insurance programs, ensuring the council identifies, assesses, and treats its material risks in accordance with the council's risk management framework, LGR s207, and ISO 31000:2018.
Key accountability 3: Lead the annual review and update of the enterprise risk management framework, including the risk appetite statement, risk assessment methodology, and risk reporting framework, ensuring alignment with QAO better practice expectations and presentation to the Audit and Risk Committee.
Essential selection criterion: Demonstrated experience (minimum five years) in leading an enterprise risk management function in a complex public sector or regulated environment, including the development and implementation of risk frameworks, risk appetite statements, and executive-level risk reporting.

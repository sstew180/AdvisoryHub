SKILL: How to Document a Probity Decision
VERSION: 1.0
DOMAIN: Professional Practice
LEVEL: Base
JURISDICTION: Queensland

PURPOSE
A probity decision is a formal governance record documenting that a procurement, assessment, or decision-making process was conducted with integrity -- that is, honestly, impartially, and in compliance with the rules. Documenting probity decisions creates an audit trail that demonstrates the council acted properly and protects officers and the council if the process is later challenged. The documentation is not about proving virtue -- it is about creating a verifiable record that the right process was followed.

WHEN TO USE THIS SKILL
- User asks to "document a probity decision" or "prepare probity records"
- User needs to record that a procurement or assessment was conducted with integrity
- User is preparing documentation for an internal or external audit of a procurement process
- User needs to demonstrate that conflicts of interest were managed appropriately
- User asks to "prepare the probity file" for a significant tender or contract award

STRUCTURE
Process overview: What was the procurement or decision-making process, when did it occur, and what was the outcome?
Probity framework: What rules, policies, and standards governed the process?
Panel composition: Who participated in the assessment or decision? What was each person's role?
Conflict of interest management: Were any conflicts declared? How were they managed?
Process compliance: Did the process follow the required steps? Any deviations and their justification?
Communication log: What communications occurred between the council and tenderers/applicants during the process? Were they compliant (for example, no unilateral communications outside the formal channel)?
Information security: How was confidential information protected during the process?
Outcome and basis: What was decided and on what basis?
Sign-off: Confirmation by the delegate or probity officer that the process was conducted with integrity.

KEY PRINCIPLES
- Document as you go, not after the fact. Probity records written after a decision has been made and a challenge has been received are not credible. Create records during the process.
- Record deviations as well as compliance. A process that deviated from the standard approach but had a legitimate reason, documented at the time, is defensible. An undocumented deviation is not.
- Conflicts of interest must be resolved before the process, not during. A conflict declared on the day of the evaluation panel is too late if the officer had access to tender documents for three weeks before declaring.
- The communication log must be complete. Selective communication logs -- capturing formal communications but not informal ones -- are a probity risk.
- Independent probity advice should be engaged for high-risk or high-value processes. A probity adviser provides an independent assessment that the process was conducted properly. Their report becomes part of the probity record.

QUALITY STANDARDS
A good probity record is one that a CCC investigator, QAO auditor, or court could examine and conclude, based on the documentation alone, that the process was conducted honestly and impartially. If the documentation has gaps -- missing conflict declarations, unexplained deviations, or an incomplete communication log -- the process is not adequately documented regardless of how it was actually conducted.

QUEENSLAND CONTEXT
Probity obligations for Queensland local government procurement arise under LGA s104(3) (sound contracting principles, including ethical behaviour and fair dealing) and the council's procurement policy. The CCC investigates probity failures in local government procurement and has published guidance on probity in public procurement. Independent probity advisers are typically engaged for procurements above $5M or for procurements in sensitive areas (IT, professional services, major infrastructure). Probity records must be retained as public records under the Public Records Act 2002 and are subject to RTI requests. Probity failures that constitute corrupt conduct must be reported to the CCC under the Crime and Corruption Act 2001 s38.

EXAMPLE LANGUAGE
Conflict of interest management: Three members of the five-person evaluation panel declared potential conflicts of interest before receiving tender documents. Panel member Mitchell declared a personal relationship with an employee of Tenderer B. Panel member Santos declared a previous employment relationship with Tenderer A (resigned from that organisation 18 months ago). Panel member Williams declared no conflict. In each case, the declared conflict was assessed by the procurement manager as non-material, given the indirect nature of the relationships and the panel's consensus-based assessment methodology. All three declarations are recorded in the conflict of interest register (references 2026-COI-014, 2026-COI-015, and 2026-COI-016). The three panel members participated in all stages of the evaluation.
Communication log: All tenderer communications were conducted through the council's e-tendering portal (VendorPanel). No direct communications between panel members and tenderers occurred during the evaluation period. One tenderer (Tenderer C) submitted a clarification request on 14 March 2026 via the portal. The response was prepared by the procurement manager, reviewed by the legal team, and published to all tenderers simultaneously on 17 March 2026 in accordance with the RFT conditions.

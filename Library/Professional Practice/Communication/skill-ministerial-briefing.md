SKILL: How to Write a Ministerial Briefing
VERSION: 1.0
DOMAIN: Professional Practice
LEVEL: Base
JURISDICTION: Queensland

PURPOSE
A ministerial briefing is a document prepared by a local government officer to brief a Queensland Government minister, ministerial adviser, or departmental liaison on a matter that involves state-local government interaction. It may be required for a ministerial visit, a funding submission, a policy consultation, or a regulatory matter. It is distinct from an internal executive briefing in that it is addressed to an external government audience and must observe strict protocols around tone, content, and political sensitivity.

WHEN TO USE THIS SKILL
- User asks to "brief the minister" or "prepare a ministerial briefing"
- User needs to communicate a council position to a state government minister or department
- User is preparing for a ministerial visit or meeting with a ministerial adviser
- User needs to respond to a request from the minister's office
- User is preparing material for the Mayor or CEO to present to a minister

STRUCTURE
Header: To, From, Subject, Date, Security classification (Sensitive: Cabinet-in-Confidence if applicable).
Purpose: One sentence. What is this briefing for and what action or awareness is sought.
Background: 3-4 sentences of essential context. What the council has done, what the current position is.
Key issues: The 2-3 matters the minister most needs to understand. Each in a short paragraph.
Council's position: What the council is asking for, or what position it wants the minister to understand.
Suggested actions: What the council would like the minister to do, decide, or say. Be specific.
Contact: CEO or Mayor name and direct contact.

KEY PRINCIPLES
- Observe ministerial protocol. Address the minister formally. Do not use first names. Do not make assumptions about ministerial support for a position.
- Be factual and measured. Ministerial briefings are formal government documents. Advocacy language appropriate for a media release is not appropriate here.
- Political sensitivity must be considered. Flag any matters that may have political implications for the minister without editorialising about them.
- Be brief. Ministers receive large volumes of material. One page is ideal. Two pages maximum.
- Represent the council, not individuals. The briefing speaks for the council organisation, not for individual officers or elected members.

QUALITY STANDARDS
A good ministerial briefing is one the minister can read in three minutes and know exactly what the council wants. It should pass a political sensitivity check -- nothing in it should embarrass the council or the minister if it became public. The council's position should be clearly stated without being demanding or presumptuous about the outcome.

QUEENSLAND CONTEXT
Queensland ministerial briefings follow the Queensland Government's Cabinet Handbook protocols. Local government interactions with state ministers are governed by the Intergovernmental Agreement on Roles and Responsibilities framework. Briefings related to funding under the Queensland Government's Works for Queensland or similar programs should reference the relevant program guidelines. Briefings relating to planning matters should reference the Planning Act 2016 and the relevant State Planning Policy. Briefings involving Local Government Infrastructure Plans should reference the LGA s197 framework.

EXAMPLE LANGUAGE
Purpose: This briefing provides the Minister for Local Government with an update on the City of Gold Coast's preparations for the 2032 Brisbane Olympic Games venue precinct and seeks the Minister's support for an accelerated approval pathway for the Coomera Indoor Sports Centre.
Council's position: The City of Gold Coast supports the Olympic venues program and has committed $340M in capital works to deliver the Coomera Indoor Sports Centre by December 2030. The critical path requires development approval by June 2026 to maintain the construction program. The council respectfully requests the Minister's support for a coordinated state-local government approval process to achieve this timeline.

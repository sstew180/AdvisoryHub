SKILL: How to Prepare Terms of Reference
VERSION: 1.0
DOMAIN: Professional Practice
LEVEL: Base
JURISDICTION: Queensland

PURPOSE
Terms of reference (ToR) define the purpose, scope, membership, and operating rules of a committee, working group, review, or project. They are the governing document that tells members what they are there to do, what authority they have, and how they will operate. Poorly drafted ToR are the most common cause of scope disputes, membership conflicts, and committees that cannot make decisions.

WHEN TO USE THIS SKILL
- User asks to "prepare terms of reference" for a committee, working group, or review
- User needs to establish a new governance body or project group
- User is reviewing or updating an existing committee's ToR
- User needs to define the scope of an independent review or investigation
- User asks to "set up a steering committee" or "charter a working group"

STRUCTURE
Name: The formal name of the committee, group, or review.
Purpose: Why this body exists. One to three sentences.
Objectives: The specific outcomes this body is expected to achieve.
Scope: What is within the remit of this body and what is explicitly excluded.
Authority: What decisions this body can make, what it can recommend but not decide, and what is outside its authority entirely.
Membership: Who sits on this body, in what capacity (voting or advisory), and how members are appointed or replaced.
Chair: Who chairs the body and what the chair's specific responsibilities are.
Meeting frequency and quorum: How often the body meets, what constitutes a quorum, and how decisions are made.
Reporting: Who this body reports to, how often, and in what format.
Secretariat: Who provides administrative support.
Review: When these ToR will be reviewed.
Approval: Who approved these ToR and when.

KEY PRINCIPLES
- Authority must be explicit. A committee that does not know whether it can make decisions or only recommendations will be paralysed or will overstep its remit.
- Scope must define exclusions as well as inclusions. What is out of scope is often more important than what is in scope.
- Membership must reflect purpose. A committee that is too large cannot make decisions. A committee that excludes key stakeholders will have its decisions challenged.
- Quorum must be achievable. A quorum requirement that is so high that meetings regularly fail to achieve it makes the committee ineffective.
- ToR must be followed. A committee that operates outside its ToR -- making decisions it has no authority to make, or with members who should not be voting -- creates governance risk.

QUALITY STANDARDS
A good ToR document is one that a new committee member can read before their first meeting and understand exactly what the committee does, what authority they have, and how they are expected to contribute. If a committee member has to ask "can we actually decide this?" at a meeting, the ToR are inadequate.

QUEENSLAND CONTEXT
Terms of reference for Queensland local government committees must be consistent with the council's governance framework and the Local Government Act 2009. The Audit and Risk Committee ToR must comply with LGR s208-211 requirements for composition, independence, and terms. Council advisory committees established under LGA s264 must have ToR consistent with the Act. Committees with external members must address conflicts of interest, confidentiality, and privacy obligations in the ToR. The council's committee framework policy should be referenced. For committees with decision-making authority, the ToR should identify the delegated authority under which decisions are made and the applicable LGA s257 delegation.

EXAMPLE LANGUAGE
Authority: The ERP Project Steering Committee has authority to: approve changes to project scope within the approved budget; approve extensions to project milestones of up to 30 days; direct the project manager on prioritisation of resources. The Steering Committee does not have authority to: approve budget variations above the approved project budget (requires Finance Committee approval); approve scope changes that alter the project's agreed business case outcomes (requires CEO approval); approve contract variations above $100,000 (requires the relevant financial delegate).
Quorum: A quorum consists of the Chair or Deputy Chair and at least two other voting members. If a quorum is not achieved, the meeting may proceed as an information session only. No formal decisions may be made and matters requiring decision must be deferred to the next scheduled meeting or resolved by circular resolution with quorum participation.

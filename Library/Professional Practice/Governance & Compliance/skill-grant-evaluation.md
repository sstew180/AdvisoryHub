SKILL: How to Evaluate a Grant Opportunity
VERSION: 1.0
DOMAIN: Professional Practice
LEVEL: Base
JURISDICTION: Queensland

PURPOSE
A grant opportunity evaluation assesses whether a particular grant program is worth pursuing, whether the council's proposed project is likely to be competitive, and what the conditions of the grant would mean for the council if the application were successful. Many councils apply for grants they are unlikely to win or that impose conditions they cannot comply with. A structured evaluation before committing to an application saves significant officer time and avoids accepting grant funding with unaffordable conditions.

WHEN TO USE THIS SKILL
- User asks to "evaluate a grant opportunity" or "should we apply for this grant"
- User needs to assess whether a council project is eligible and competitive for a specific grant
- User is preparing a recommendation to pursue or not pursue a grant application
- User needs to understand the conditions attached to a grant before accepting
- User asks to "assess this funding opportunity" for a council program or project

STRUCTURE
Grant overview: Program name, funding body, total pool, maximum grant amount, and closing date.
Eligibility assessment: Is the council eligible? Is the proposed project eligible? Any exclusions that apply?
Alignment assessment: How well does the proposed project align with the grant's stated objectives and priorities?
Competitiveness assessment: How competitive is the council's project likely to be? What is the funding ratio (applications to grants)?
Conditions assessment: What conditions would apply if the grant were awarded? Co-contribution requirements, reporting obligations, acquittal requirements, timing constraints.
Resource assessment: What officer time and cost would be required to prepare a competitive application?
Recommendation: Apply, do not apply, or apply with modifications to the project scope.

KEY PRINCIPLES
- Assess eligibility before anything else. An ineligible application wastes everyone's time. Confirm eligibility against the program guidelines before investing in the assessment.
- Alignment drives competitiveness. Grants are awarded to projects that best meet the program's objectives, not the council's objectives. Frame the project in terms of what the grant program is trying to achieve.
- Conditions can make a grant unaffordable. A $500,000 grant that requires $750,000 in co-contribution, quarterly reporting for five years, and asset retention for 20 years may cost more than it delivers. Always read the conditions before recommending an application.
- Timing must be realistic. A grant that requires construction to commence within six months may not be achievable if the project still requires development approval.
- The cost of application must be proportionate. A complex grant application that takes 80 officer hours to prepare for a $50,000 grant may not represent value for money.

QUALITY STANDARDS
A good grant opportunity evaluation gives the decision-maker a clear recommendation with a rationale they can act on. The eligibility assessment must be definitive -- "probably eligible" is not good enough for a recommendation to invest significant officer time. The conditions assessment must be thorough -- conditions that are not identified before the application is lodged become binding if the grant is awarded.

QUEENSLAND CONTEXT
Grant applications for Queensland local governments must be authorised by the appropriate delegate under LGA s257. Applications that commit the council to co-contribution expenditure must have that expenditure identified in the approved budget or a budget amendment approved before the application is lodged. Grants from state and federal governments typically require acquittal reports and are subject to audit by the funding body. The council's grants register should be updated when a grant application is lodged and when a decision is received. Grant conditions involving land use or infrastructure must be assessed against the council's asset management obligations. Commonwealth grants may have procurement requirements (AusTender, Commonwealth Procurement Rules) that differ from the council's own procurement framework.

EXAMPLE LANGUAGE
Eligibility assessment: The council is eligible as a local government body. The proposed Burleigh Heads district park project is eligible as a community infrastructure project. However, the project would not be eligible if it includes the playground equipment replacement component -- the guidelines specifically exclude replacement of existing assets. If the application proceeds, the scope should be limited to the new amenities building and car park.
Conditions assessment: The program requires a minimum 20% council co-contribution ($240,000 on a $1.2M project). This is affordable within the approved capital budget. The program requires quarterly progress reports for two years, an audit of expenditure at completion, and asset retention for 15 years. The asset retention condition is consistent with the council's asset management plan. The quarterly reporting obligation will require approximately four hours of officer time per quarter for two years. No conditions are assessed as unaffordable or unachievable.
Recommendation: Apply. The project is eligible, well-aligned with the program objectives, and the conditions are manageable. The estimated officer time for the application is 20 hours. The competitive ratio for this program in the prior round was approximately 1 in 3, and the council's project is considered above-average competitive given its location in a high-growth area and the absence of existing district park facilities within 5km.

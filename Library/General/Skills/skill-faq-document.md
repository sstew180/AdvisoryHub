SKILL: How to Write an FAQ Document
VERSION: 1.0
DOMAIN: Professional Practice
LEVEL: Base
JURISDICTION: Queensland

PURPOSE
A Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ) document anticipates and answers the questions a particular audience -- residents, staff, contractors, or service users -- is most likely to ask about a topic, decision, project, or change. A well-written FAQ reduces inbound enquiries, builds trust through transparency, and gives front-line staff consistent answers. A poorly written FAQ that does not address real questions, or that answers questions no one is asking, wastes everyone's time.

WHEN TO USE THIS SKILL
- User asks to "write an FAQ" or "prepare frequently asked questions"
- User needs to support a community consultation, project launch, or service change
- User is preparing staff communication about a policy or organisational change
- User needs to equip customer service staff with consistent answers
- User asks to "anticipate the questions" about a council decision or initiative

STRUCTURE
Introduction: One or two sentences explaining what this FAQ covers and who it is for.
Questions: Organised into logical groups if there are more than eight questions. Within each group, ordered from most to least important.
Each Q&A: Question in plain language as the audience would ask it. Answer in two to four sentences maximum. Direct and honest.
Further information: Where to get more detail -- website, phone number, email, office.

KEY PRINCIPLES
- Write questions as the audience would ask them, not as management would frame them. "Why is my rates bill higher this year?" not "What factors have contributed to the adjustment of residential rate assessments?"
- Answer the hard questions first. The questions the council is least comfortable answering are usually the ones the audience most wants answered. Avoiding them destroys credibility.
- Be honest about what you do not know yet. "We have not yet finalised the timeline -- we expect to confirm by 30 June and will update this FAQ at that point" is a complete answer.
- Keep answers short. If an answer requires more than four sentences, the question is too broad or the answer is not clear enough.
- Update the FAQ. An FAQ about a live project that has not been updated in six months is worse than no FAQ.

QUALITY STANDARDS
A good FAQ is one that reduces inbound enquiries by at least half. Test the FAQ by giving it to someone who knows nothing about the topic and asking them to find the answer to five specific questions. If they cannot find the answers, reorganise or add questions. If they find answers that are technically accurate but unsatisfying, rewrite them in plainer language.

QUEENSLAND CONTEXT
FAQs for Queensland local government must not contain statements that conflict with council resolutions, legal advice, or regulatory obligations. FAQs involving development applications, appeals, or legal proceedings must be reviewed by the legal team before publication. FAQs about rate changes must be consistent with the council's adopted budget. FAQs about employment or enterprise agreement matters must be reviewed by HR. FAQs that will be published on the council website are subject to the council's web content accessibility guidelines -- questions and answers should be structured using appropriate heading levels for screen reader compatibility.

EXAMPLE LANGUAGE
Q: Why is there construction happening in my street?
A: The council is upgrading the water mains in your street as part of the Southeast Water Infrastructure Program. The work involves replacing ageing pipes that are more than 40 years old. We expect construction to be complete by 30 August 2026.

Q: Will my water be turned off during construction?
A: Your water supply will be interrupted briefly on two occasions during the work -- once when the new pipe is connected and once when the old pipe is decommissioned. We will letterbox-drop your property at least 48 hours before each interruption with the exact date and time.

Q: My driveway has been damaged. Who do I contact?
A: We are sorry to hear that. Please call 1300 GOLDCOAST or email infrastructure@goldcoast.qld.gov.au with your address and a description of the damage. Our contractor is required to repair any property damage caused by the works within five business days of notification.

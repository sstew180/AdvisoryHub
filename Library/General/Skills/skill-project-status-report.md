SKILL: How to Write a Project Status Report
VERSION: 1.0
DOMAIN: General
LEVEL: Base
JURISDICTION: Queensland

PURPOSE
A project status report is a regular, structured update that tells a project sponsor, steering committee, or executive what is happening on a project, whether it is on track, and what decisions or actions are needed. It is not a progress diary. Its purpose is to surface issues early, flag risks, and keep decision-makers informed without requiring them to attend every project meeting.

WHEN TO USE THIS SKILL
- User asks to "write a project status report" or "prepare a project update"
- User asks to "update the steering committee on the project"
- User needs to report to an executive sponsor on project progress
- User asks to "prepare the monthly project report" or "weekly update"
- User needs to escalate a project issue through a formal reporting channel

STRUCTURE
Project name and reporting period: Clear header identifying the project and the period covered.
Overall status: A single RAG rating (Red / Amber / Green) with a one-sentence explanation.
Progress this period: What was completed. Specific deliverables or milestones achieved, not activities.
Planned next period: What is scheduled for the next reporting period. Specific and measurable.
Issues: Problems currently affecting the project. Each issue should include: what it is, its impact, and the proposed resolution.
Risks: Key risks that may affect the project going forward. Each risk should include: the risk, likelihood, consequence, and current mitigation.
Decisions required: Any decisions the sponsor or committee needs to make. Be specific about what is needed, from whom, and by when.
Financial status: Budget position — approved budget, actual spend to date, forecast at completion, variance.

KEY PRINCIPLES
- The RAG status must be justified. "Green" when the project is a week behind schedule is dishonest. Use Amber early rather than holding Green until a crisis forces Red.
- Report on outcomes, not activity. "Completed the contractor briefing session with 12 attendees" is activity. "Contractor mobilisation completed, site works commence 21 April" is an outcome.
- Issues and risks are different. An issue is something happening now that is affecting the project. A risk is something that might happen and has not yet materialised.
- Decisions required must be specific. Do not include items on this list unless a named person needs to do something by a specific date.
- Financial status must be current. A status report with stale financial data is unreliable.

QUALITY STANDARDS
A good project status report can be read by the sponsor in five minutes and gives them a clear picture of where the project stands, what problems exist, and what they need to do. If the sponsor has to ask follow-up questions to understand the financial position or the key issues, the report has failed. RAG ratings must be applied consistently across reporting periods — a project that goes from Green to Red in one reporting period without passing through Amber has not been reported honestly.

QUEENSLAND CONTEXT
Project status reports for Queensland local government should reflect the council's project management framework and any gateway review requirements. For capital works projects, reporting should align with the Queensland Government's project assurance framework where applicable. Financial reporting should reference the approved council budget and any s50 budget amendments. For significant projects, reporting to the ARC or Finance Committee may be required under the council's governance framework. Project risks should be captured in or linked to the council's risk register where they meet the threshold for strategic or operational risk.

EXAMPLE LANGUAGE
Overall status: AMBER — the design documentation review has been delayed by two weeks due to resource constraints at the hydraulic engineering consultant. This creates a four-week float risk against the June 2026 construction start date.
Issues: Design review delay. The hydraulic engineering consultant has advised they cannot complete the design review by 30 April as contracted. Impact: construction start date moves from 2 June to 30 June if not resolved. Proposed resolution: Director Infrastructure to approve engagement of a second reviewer to work in parallel. Decision required by 25 April.
Financial status: Approved budget $4.2M. Actual spend to date $1.1M (26%). Forecast at completion $4.35M. Variance $150,000 unfavourable — driven by increased material costs since contract award. Budget amendment to be submitted to May Finance Committee.

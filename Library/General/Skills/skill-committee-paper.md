SKILL: How to Prepare a Committee or Board Paper
VERSION: 1.0
DOMAIN: General
LEVEL: Base
JURISDICTION: Queensland

PURPOSE
A committee or board paper is a formal document submitted to a governance body — such as an Audit and Risk Committee, Council, Finance Committee, or Board — to seek a decision, note a matter, or receive a report. It is the primary mechanism through which officers present recommendations to those charged with governance. A well-constructed paper gives the committee the information it needs to make an informed decision without requiring supplementary explanation from the author.

WHEN TO USE THIS SKILL
- User asks to "prepare a committee paper" or "write a board paper"
- User asks to "take this to the ARC" or "prepare a council report"
- User asks to "write a paper for the Finance Committee" or similar governance body
- User needs to seek approval, note a matter, or provide an update through a formal governance channel
- User asks to "prepare a submission" to an internal committee or working group

STRUCTURE
Header: Committee name, meeting date, agenda item number, paper title, author, date, security classification.
Executive summary: 3-5 sentences. The recommendation and the key reason for it. A busy committee member should be able to read this and know what they are being asked to decide.
Background: Essential context only. What led to this paper being presented. Not a history lesson.
Analysis or findings: The substance of the paper. What has been assessed, what options were considered, what the risks are.
Recommendation: What the committee is being asked to resolve, note, or approve. Use precise language — "That the Committee resolves to approve..." or "That the Committee notes...".
Financial implications: Cost, funding source, and budget impact. Mandatory even if the answer is nil.
Risk implications: Key risks associated with the recommendation and proposed mitigations.
Attachments: List only. Do not embed attachments in the paper body.

KEY PRINCIPLES
- The recommendation must appear twice: in the executive summary and as a formal resolution at the end.
- Financial and risk implications are not optional. Every paper must address both, even if the answer is nil or not applicable.
- Write the analysis to support the recommendation, not to present all possible information. The committee is not a research audience.
- Use plain language. Technical terms must be explained or avoided.
- Papers should be self-contained. A committee member who has not attended previous meetings should be able to understand the paper without briefing.

QUALITY STANDARDS
A good committee paper is one the committee can act on without supplementary explanation. If the chair has to ask "what exactly are we being asked to decide?" the paper has failed. The recommendation should be precise enough to appear verbatim in the minutes. Financial and risk sections should contain actual figures and specific risks, not placeholders or generalities.

QUEENSLAND CONTEXT
Committee papers for Queensland local government bodies must comply with the Local Government Act 2009 requirements for transparency and accountability. Papers going to Council must be prepared in accordance with the local government's meeting procedures local law. The Audit and Risk Committee's role is defined in LGR s208-211 — ARC papers should stay within the committee's terms of reference and not seek decisions outside its authority. Where a recommendation involves expenditure, the delegated authority or council resolution requirement under LGA s257 must be identified. Papers containing information that may be considered confidential must be assessed against the LGA s254J grounds for closing a meeting.

EXAMPLE LANGUAGE
Recommendation: That the Audit and Risk Committee resolves to endorse the updated Enterprise Risk Management Framework (version 3.1, dated 14 April 2026) and recommend it to Council for adoption at the May 2026 ordinary meeting.
Financial implications: The revised framework does not require additional budget allocation. Implementation will be delivered within existing operational budgets for the Risk and Insurance team ($420,000 recurrent).
Risk implications: The primary risk of not adopting the revised framework is non-compliance with the QAO better practice expectations outlined in the 2024-25 management letter. Residual risk is assessed as moderate pending Council adoption.

SKILL: How to Write a Budget Variance Explanation
VERSION: 1.0
DOMAIN: Professional Practice
LEVEL: Base
JURISDICTION: Queensland

PURPOSE
A budget variance explanation is a written account of why actual financial results differ from the approved budget. It is required for committee reporting, executive oversight, and audit purposes. A good variance explanation tells the reader what happened, why it happened, and what will happen next. A poor one describes the variance in accounting terms without explaining its cause or implications.

WHEN TO USE THIS SKILL
- User asks to "explain the budget variance" or "write up the variance commentary"
- User needs to prepare the financial commentary for a committee report
- User needs to respond to a question about why spending is over or under budget
- User is preparing the narrative section of a monthly or quarterly financial report
- User asks to "explain why we are over budget" or "why are we underspent"

STRUCTURE
Variance statement: The size and direction of the variance in dollar terms and as a percentage of budget.
Cause: The specific reason or reasons for the variance. One cause per paragraph.
Nature of the variance: Is it a timing difference (will correct itself), a permanent variance (will not correct), or an emerging risk (may get worse)?
Forecast impact: What the full-year position is expected to be.
Management action: What is being done about an unfavourable variance, or why no action is needed for a favourable one.

KEY PRINCIPLES
- State the cause, not the symptom. "Expenditure is above budget due to higher costs" is a description of the variance, not its cause. "Expenditure is above budget due to a 14% increase in fuel costs since the budget was set in June 2025, driven by increased global oil prices" is a cause.
- Distinguish timing from structural. Readers need to know whether a variance will self-correct or whether it represents a genuine budget problem. State explicitly which type it is.
- Quantify the forecast. A variance explanation that does not state the expected full-year impact leaves the reader to guess. Always include a full-year forecast.
- Be specific about management action. "We are monitoring the situation" is not a management action. "We have deferred three planned maintenance activities to Q4 to partially offset the cost increase, reducing the forecast unfavourable variance from $280,000 to $190,000" is an action.
- Write for the committee member, not the accountant. Variance explanations in committee reports are read by councillors and non-financial executives. Plain language is required.

QUALITY STANDARDS
A good variance explanation gives a Finance Committee member everything they need to understand the variance and decide whether it requires further attention. If a committee member reads the explanation and still asks "but why is it over budget?" the explanation has not answered the question. The forecast position must be clearly stated and must be based on a realistic assessment, not an optimistic one.

QUEENSLAND CONTEXT
Budget variance explanations for Queensland local government are included in monthly financial reports presented to the Finance Committee or equivalent governance body. Significant variances -- typically defined as greater than $100,000 or 5% of budget, whichever is lower -- require specific commentary. Variances that will result in a budget being exceeded require a budget amendment under LGR s50 before the expenditure is incurred, not after. Explanations involving capital works variances should reference the project schedule and indicate whether the variance is a delivery risk or a phasing adjustment. The QAO reviews variance explanations as part of its annual financial audit and expects them to be substantive and accurate.

EXAMPLE LANGUAGE
Favourable variance: Employee costs are $340,000 favourable to budget year to date. This is primarily a timing variance resulting from three positions that were budgeted to be filled from 1 July 2025 but have not yet been filled due to a competitive labour market for specialist risk and compliance roles. Two positions are currently at final interview stage and are expected to be filled by 1 May. The third position has been redesigned and will be readvertised in April. Full-year forecast: $180,000 favourable, reflecting the expected commencement dates of the three roles.
Unfavourable variance -- structural: Legal costs are $220,000 unfavourable to budget year to date. This variance will not self-correct. The budget of $450,000 was set based on prior-year actuals but did not account for two significant development application disputes that were filed in September 2025. The council's solicitors have estimated costs to resolution of approximately $380,000 for these two matters. A budget amendment of $350,000 will be submitted to the Finance Committee at the April meeting to reflect the revised forecast of $800,000.

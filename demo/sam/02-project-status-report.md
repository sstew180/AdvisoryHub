## Issues Requiring Action

### Issue 1 -- Coomera Road Widening: Contractor Variation Claim (HIGH PRIORITY)
**Summary:** BuildRight Civil Pty Ltd lodged a variation claim on 22 March 2026 for $380,000 related to unexpected rock encountered during excavation at chainage 1.4km. The claim references the contract's latent conditions clause (Clause 18.3).

**Assessment in progress:** The organisation's quantity surveyor (QS) is assessing the claim. Preliminary view is that $180,000-$220,000 may be substantiated under the latent conditions clause. The remaining $160,000-$200,000 appears to relate to contractor productivity issues not attributable to the latent condition.

**Timeline:** QS assessment due 18 April. Negotiation with contractor April-May. Target: resolve by 31 May to avoid formal dispute process.

**Budget impact:** If variation is settled at $200,000, the project will be $200,000 over its approved budget and a budget amendment will be required. If settled at $380,000 as claimed, a $380,000 amendment will be required.

**Action required:** Director Infrastructure to approve QS engagement to assess claim and authorise Jordan Clarke to negotiate on behalf of organisation. Decision needed by 14 April.

### Issue 2 -- Bridge Renewal: Ergon Energy Utility Conflict (HIGH PRIORITY)
**Summary:** Survey work for the Bridge 2 deck renewal (Jacobs Well Road) identified an Ergon Energy underground cable running through the bridge structure at a depth inconsistent with as-built records. Construction cannot proceed until the cable is relocated or a protective design is confirmed.

**Status:** Ergon Energy notified 14 February 2026. Ergon has acknowledged the issue but has not confirmed a resolution timeline. Current estimate from Ergon: 8-12 weeks from design confirmation.

**Impact:** Construction start pushed from April to June 2026 at earliest. Full-year capital delivery for this project now at risk. If Ergon's timeline extends beyond June, the project will not be completed in 2025-26.

**Budget impact:** Design changes and potential cable protection works estimated at $45,000-$95,000 additional cost. Ergon's responsibility under the road occupancy agreement is being assessed by legal team.

**Action required:** Escalate to Director Infrastructure for direct engagement with Ergon Energy General Manager. Legal team to advise on cost recovery options by 21 April.

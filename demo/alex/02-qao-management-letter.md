## Extract: Risk Management (Section 4)

**Rating scale:** Significant / Moderate / Minor

## Finding 4.1 — Risk Appetite Statement Not Current (Significant)

**Observation:**
The organisation's risk appetite statement was last updated in March 2021. Since that time, the operating environment has changed materially, including the commencement of the $2.1 billion Olympic Games infrastructure program, the Digital Transformation Strategy, and significant workforce changes post-COVID. The current risk appetite statement does not reflect the organisation's current risk profile or strategic priorities.

During our testing, we found that six of twelve business units were unable to demonstrate how their risk registers had been assessed against the current risk appetite. Three business units were operating with risk registers that had not been reviewed in more than 18 months.

**Risk:**
Without a current and understood risk appetite statement, the organisation cannot ensure that risk-taking is consistent with its strategic objectives and governance obligations. Material risks may be accepted or rejected inconsistently across business units.

**Recommendation:**
Management should update the risk appetite statement to reflect the current operating environment and strategic priorities. The updated statement should be endorsed by the Audit and Risk Committee and communicated to all business units. Risk registers should be assessed against the updated statement within 90 days of endorsement.

**Management response:**
Agreed. The Manager Risk and Insurance will lead an Enterprise Risk Framework Review commencing February 2026. An updated risk appetite statement will be presented to the ARC for endorsement by August 2026. Target completion: 30 June 2026.

**Responsible officer:** Manager Risk and Insurance
**Target date:** 30 June 2026

## Finding 4.2 — Risk Reporting to ARC Lacks Operational Detail (Significant)

**Observation:**
The quarterly risk report presented to the Audit and Risk Committee does not provide sufficient information for the Committee to fulfil its oversight role. Specifically:

- The report lists 47 strategic risks but provides treatment status for only 18 (38%)
- Emerging risks identified during the quarter are not systematically included
- The report does not distinguish between risks where treatment is on track and those where treatment has stalled
- There is no trend analysis showing whether the overall risk profile is improving or deteriorating

We compared the ARC risk report to the QAO better practice standard for risk reporting to governance bodies. The current report meets 4 of 9 better practice criteria.

**Risk:**
The ARC cannot effectively discharge its oversight responsibilities if it does not receive complete and accurate information about the organisation's risk profile and the effectiveness of risk treatment.

**Recommendation:**
Management should redesign the ARC risk report to: (1) include treatment status for all high and extreme risks; (2) include an emerging risks section; (3) include a trend indicator for each risk; and (4) include an overall risk profile indicator showing movement from the prior period.

**Management response:**
Agreed. The redesigned risk report will be implemented from the Q2 2025-26 ARC meeting (February 2026). The Manager Risk and Insurance will develop a revised reporting template as part of the ERM Framework Review.

**Responsible officer:** Manager Risk and Insurance
**Target date:** February 2026 (first revised report)

## Finding 4.3 — Risk Management Capability Inconsistent Across Business Units (Significant)

**Observation:**
Our interviews with risk champions across eight business units identified significant inconsistency in risk management capability and practice. Key issues identified:

- Four business units do not have a designated risk champion
- Risk champions in three business units have received no formal risk management training in the past three years
- Risk assessment methodology is applied inconsistently -- two business units use a 3x3 matrix rather than the organisation's standard 5x5 matrix
- Risk workshops are conducted annually in six business units but ad hoc or not at all in four business units

The inconsistency in capability and practice means that the organisation's consolidated risk register does not provide a reliable picture of the risk profile across all business units.

**Risk:**
Inconsistent risk management capability creates gaps in the organisation's overall risk coverage. Material risks in poorly-performing business units may not be identified, assessed, or treated effectively.

**Recommendation:**
Management should: (1) ensure all business units have a designated risk champion; (2) implement a mandatory annual training program for risk champions; (3) enforce consistent use of the 5x5 risk assessment methodology; and (4) establish a minimum standard for risk workshop frequency (at least semi-annual).

**Management response:**
Agreed. The ERM Framework Review will address all four elements of this recommendation. The capability uplift program will be developed and delivered as part of the review. Mandatory training for all risk champions will be implemented by 30 June 2026.

**Responsible officer:** Manager Risk and Insurance
**Target date:** 30 June 2026

# Enterprise Risk Framework Review — Current State Assessment
**Prepared by:** Meridian Risk Advisory Pty Ltd
**Date:** 28 March 2026
**Version:** 1.0 (draft for management review)

---

## Executive Summary

This current state assessment evaluates the organisation's enterprise risk management practices against ISO 31000:2018 and the QAO better practice risk management standard. The assessment is based on document review, interviews with 11 of 12 business unit risk champions, and analysis of the enterprise risk register and ARC reporting.

**Overall maturity rating: Developing (Level 2 of 4)**

The organisation has foundational risk management structures in place -- a risk policy, a risk register, and an ARC reporting cycle -- but practices are inconsistent, the framework is outdated, and governance reporting does not meet better practice standards. The three QAO management letter findings (rated Significant) are consistent with our assessment.

---

## Assessment Against ISO 31000:2018 Principles

| Principle | Rating | Key Observations |
|---|---|---|
| Integrated | Developing | Risk management is not consistently integrated into strategic and operational planning cycles |
| Structured and comprehensive | Developing | Framework document exists but is 7 years old and does not reflect current methodology |
| Customised | Inadequate | Risk assessment methodology not tailored to different risk categories (strategic, operational, project) |
| Inclusive | Developing | Risk champions exist in 8 of 12 business units; engagement quality varies significantly |
| Dynamic | Inadequate | Risk register updates are largely annual rather than continuous; emerging risks not systematically captured |
| Best available information | Developing | Risk assessments rely on qualitative judgement; limited use of data and analytics |
| Human and cultural factors | Inadequate | No formal risk culture assessment; risk management seen as compliance exercise in 4 business units |
| Continual improvement | Developing | No formal review cycle for risk management effectiveness |

**Rating scale:** Adequate / Developing / Inadequate

## Key Gaps and Recommendations Summary

| Gap | Priority | Recommendation |
|---|---|---|
| Risk appetite statement outdated and incomplete | Critical | Update to add technology, climate, and supply chain categories. Revise strategic appetite for Olympic program context. |
| Three business units without risk champions | Critical | Appoint risk champions in Economy and Industry, Lifestyle and Community, and Olympic Games Delivery immediately. |
| 14 risks without treatment plans including 1 Extreme | Critical | Require treatment plans for all Extreme and High risks within 30 days. |
| ARC reporting does not meet better practice | High | Redesign ARC report template. Implement trend indicators and treatment status for all High and Extreme risks. |
| Inconsistent methodology (3x3 vs 5x5) | High | Mandate 5x5 methodology. Provide training to Economy and Industry business unit. |
| No risk culture program | Medium | Include risk culture assessment in capability uplift program. |
| No emerging risk process | Medium | Establish quarterly emerging risk scan as part of ERM framework. |

---

## Conclusion

The organisation's risk management framework requires substantive updating to meet ISO 31000:2018 and QAO better practice standards. The three Significant QAO findings are symptomatic of deeper structural issues rather than isolated gaps. The recommended framework update, capability uplift program, and ARC reporting redesign will address the core issues if implemented with sufficient sponsorship and pace.

The Olympic Games Delivery gap is the most urgent matter and should be addressed ahead of the broader framework review timeline.

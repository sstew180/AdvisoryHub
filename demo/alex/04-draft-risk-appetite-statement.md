## Purpose

This Risk Appetite Statement defines the nature and level of risk the organisation is willing to accept in pursuit of its strategic objectives. It provides a framework for consistent risk decision-making across all business units and programs.

This statement replaces the Risk Appetite Statement dated March 2021.

---

## Risk Appetite Framework

Risk appetite is expressed at two levels:

1. **Overall organisational appetite** -- the broad approach to risk-taking across the organisation
2. **Category-specific appetite** -- the specific appetite for each risk category

**Overall appetite statement:**
The organisation has a moderate overall risk appetite. We accept that achieving our strategic objectives -- including delivering the Olympic Games infrastructure program, executing the Digital Transformation Strategy, and maintaining service levels for our growing community -- requires informed risk-taking. We will not avoid risk where taking risk is necessary to achieve strategic outcomes. We will not accept risk that is unmanaged, unmonitored, or inconsistent with our values.

---

## Category Risk Appetite

### Strategic Risk
**Appetite: Moderate to High**

We accept strategic risk in pursuit of transformational outcomes, particularly in the context of the 2032 Olympic Games infrastructure program and Digital Transformation Strategy. We expect to take on risk at the frontier of our capability where the strategic benefit justifies it. Strategic risks must be actively managed with clear treatment plans and executive oversight.

**Tolerance:** We will not accept strategic risks rated Extreme without direct CEO and ARC oversight and an approved treatment plan. We will not accept strategic risks that threaten the organisation's financial sustainability or long-term service delivery capacity.

---

### Financial Risk
**Appetite: Low**

We are risk averse in financial management. We maintain strong budget discipline, conservative forecasting, and robust financial controls. We will not accept financial risks that threaten our operating surplus, long-term financial sustainability, or our ability to fund the capital program.

**Tolerance:** Unfavourable budget variances above 3% of operating budget require Director approval and Finance Committee notification. Capital program variances above 5% require CEO approval.

---

### Operational Risk
**Appetite: Low**

We accept that operational disruptions occur in a complex service delivery environment. We invest in prevention and in resilience to minimise the impact of disruptions when they occur. We do not accept operational failures that are preventable through reasonable controls.

**Tolerance:** Service disruptions affecting more than 10,000 residents require Director notification within 4 hours and CEO notification within 24 hours. Repeat operational failures (same cause occurring twice in 12 months) require a root cause analysis and treatment plan within 30 days.

---

### Safety Risk
**Appetite: Zero**

We have zero appetite for serious safety incidents -- both workplace safety and public safety. Safety is non-negotiable. No operational, financial, or program objective justifies accepting a safety risk that can be eliminated or adequately controlled.

**Tolerance:** Any serious injury or fatality triggers immediate CEO and Board notification. A threshold of zero tolerance applies to preventable serious injuries.

---

### Compliance and Regulatory Risk
**Appetite: Zero**

We have zero appetite for non-compliance with legislation, regulation, or mandatory standards. Compliance is a baseline expectation, not a risk to be managed against other priorities.

**Tolerance:** Any identified compliance breach must be reported to the relevant Director within 24 hours and assessed for regulatory notification obligations within 48 hours.

---

### Reputational Risk
**Appetite: Low**

We protect our reputation as a trusted, well-governed organisation. We are particularly sensitive to reputational risks in the context of the Olympic Games, where national and international scrutiny is elevated.

**Tolerance:** Any matter with potential for significant negative media coverage requires Director notification before any public comment is made. Olympic Games-related reputational risks require CEO notification.

---

### Technology and Cybersecurity Risk
**Appetite: Low**

We accept technology risk inherent in the delivery of the Digital Transformation Strategy. We do not accept cybersecurity risks that are preventable through reasonable controls. The protection of community data, critical infrastructure systems, and Olympic Games technology is a high priority.

**Tolerance:** Any cybersecurity incident affecting critical infrastructure or involving personal data of more than 1,000 individuals requires immediate CEO and Chair notification. Cybersecurity risks rated High or Extreme require a treatment plan within 30 days.

---

### Climate and Environmental Risk
**Appetite: Low to Moderate**

We accept that climate change creates material risks to our infrastructure, service delivery, and community. We are building climate resilience into our asset management and capital planning. We accept the inherent uncertainty in climate risk modelling while committing to incorporate best available science into our planning.

**Tolerance:** Climate risks must be incorporated into all major infrastructure business cases and asset management plans. Infrastructure assets with a design life exceeding 30 years must be assessed against projected climate scenarios.

---

### Supply Chain and Contractor Risk
**Appetite: Low to Moderate**

We accept supply chain risk inherent in a $1.8 billion annual capital program. We actively manage contractor performance and financial stability, particularly for the Olympic Games program. We do not accept supply chain concentration risks that are avoidable through diversified procurement strategies.

**Tolerance:** Single-source dependencies for critical services must be reported to the relevant Director and included in the risk register. Contractor financial health checks are required for all contracts above $5 million annually.

## Risk Appetite in Practice

| Situation | Expected response |
|---|---|
| A new risk is identified rated Extreme | Immediate Director notification. Treatment plan within 14 days. CEO briefing. |
| A risk treatment is overdue by more than 30 days | Escalation to Director. Reason for delay and revised date required within 5 business days. |
| A proposed initiative would create a new High risk | Business case must address the risk. Director approval required before proceeding. |
| A risk exceeds the stated tolerance | Escalate to the next governance level immediately. Do not proceed without explicit approval. |

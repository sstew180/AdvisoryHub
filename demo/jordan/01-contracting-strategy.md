**Project title:** Facilities Management Services Retender

**Version:** 2.1 (approved by Director)

---

## Executive Summary

This contracting strategy covers the procurement of Facilities Management Services for the organisation's 47 owned and leased buildings. The current contract (with Ventia Facilities Pty Ltd) expires 30 September 2026 with no further extension options. The estimated annual contract value is $6.5 million, making this a Significant Contract under the organisation's procurement policy.

**Recommended approach:** Open tender via the organisation's e-tendering platform (VendorPanel). Evaluation using a weighted criteria model. Contract term: 3 years + 2 x 1-year options (maximum 5 years).

---

## Background

### Current Contract
- **Incumbent:** Ventia Facilities Pty Ltd
- **Contract value:** $5.8 million per annum (original) / $6.2 million current (after 3 variations)
- **Contract period:** 1 October 2021 to 30 September 2026 (5 years, no extensions remaining)
- **Services covered:** Cleaning, security, building maintenance (planned and reactive), grounds maintenance, waste management, pest control, and minor works

### Why we are retendering
The current contract has performed adequately but below expectations in several areas (see Incumbent Performance Review). The market has evolved since 2021 -- integrated facilities management models, technology-enabled service delivery, and sustainability requirements are now standard expectations. The new contract must be structured to deliver better performance outcomes and value for money.

### Market context
The facilities management market in Queensland is competitive at this contract value. Key players include:
- Ventia (incumbent)
- ISS Facility Services
- Broadspectrum
- Sodexo
- BGIS
- Spotless Services (Downer Group)

Market assessment indicates 4-6 competitive submissions are likely. The $6.5M annual value and 3+2 year term (potential $32.5M total) will attract tier-1 providers.

## Scope of Services

**Core services (mandatory):**
- Building cleaning -- all 47 buildings, daily/weekly/periodic schedules
- Security -- manned guarding at 8 buildings, patrol services at all buildings, access control management
- Building maintenance -- planned preventive maintenance (PPM) for all building systems (HVAC, electrical, plumbing, fire systems, lifts)
- Reactive maintenance -- 24/7 response, 4-hour response for urgent, 24-hour for standard
- Grounds and landscaping -- all owned properties
- Waste management -- general, recycling, and green waste
- Pest control -- scheduled and reactive

**Optional services (to be priced separately):**
- Minor capital works (up to $50,000 per work order)
- Energy management and sustainability reporting
- Technology-enabled asset management (IoT sensors, predictive maintenance)

## Contract Structure

**Term:** 3 years (1 October 2026 to 30 September 2029) with 2 x 1-year options at the organisation's discretion.

**Pricing model:** 
- Fixed monthly management fee (cleaning, security, grounds, waste, pest)
- Schedule of rates for reactive maintenance (call-out fee + hourly rate + materials)
- Lump sum for PPM (annual program)
- Schedule of rates for minor works

**Price adjustment:** CPI adjustment annually on the anniversary of contract commencement (Brisbane CPI, ABS).

**Performance regime:**
- Key Performance Indicators (KPIs) with monthly reporting
- At-risk amount: 5% of monthly management fee at risk against KPI performance
- Bonus: Up to 2% of annual contract value for sustained high performance (>95% KPI achievement for 12 consecutive months)

## Evaluation Criteria

| Criteria | Weight | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Price | 30% | Total cost of ownership over 3 years including optional services |
| Technical capability and methodology | 25% | How the tenderer will deliver the services |
| Relevant experience | 20% | Evidence of similar contracts in scale and complexity |
| Key personnel | 15% | Site manager, account manager qualifications and experience |
| Sustainability and social value | 10% | Environmental management, local employment |

**Mandatory requirements (pass/fail before scored evaluation):**
- Current insurance: public liability ($20M), workers compensation, professional indemnity ($5M)
- No adverse findings from regulatory bodies in past 3 years
- Financial viability assessment (Dun & Bradstreet rating or equivalent)
- Current Safe Work Method Statements for all services

## Key Risks

| Risk | Rating | Treatment |
|---|---|---|
| Insufficient competitive market response -- fewer than 3 compliant submissions | High | Pre-tender market engagement completed (March 2026). All 6 target suppliers confirmed intention to tender. |
| Incumbent disengages during transition, creating service gap | High | Transition requirements in contract. Step-in rights during mobilisation. Director to brief Ventia CEO on expectations. |
| New contract price significantly above budget | Medium | Budget planning includes 5% contingency. If award price exceeds budget, scope reduction options identified. |
| Evaluation panel conflict of interest | Medium | Independent probity adviser engaged. COI declarations required before tender documents received. |
| Timeline slippage -- new contract not in place by 1 October | Medium | 3-week float built into timeline. Director approval to fast-track if required. |
| Scope gap -- services not adequately defined | Low | Legal review of scope. Site inspection included in tender process. |

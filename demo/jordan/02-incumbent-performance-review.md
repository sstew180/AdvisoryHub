# Incumbent Performance Review — Ventia Facilities Pty Ltd
**Contract:** Facilities Management Services (2026-FAC-001 predecessor)
**Review period:** October 2021 to February 2026 (4.5 years)
**Prepared by:** Jordan Clarke, Senior Contracts Officer
**Date:** February 2026

---

## Executive Summary

Ventia Facilities Pty Ltd has performed adequately under the Facilities Management Services contract but has consistently delivered below the KPI targets in three of seven performance areas. Contract value has grown from $5.8M to $6.2M per annum through approved variations. The relationship has been professionally managed but responsiveness to reactive maintenance has been a persistent issue. The organisation should not restrict the retender to Ventia as incumbent and should ensure the new contract's KPI regime and pricing structure addresses the shortfalls identified in this review.

**Overall performance rating: Satisfactory (below contract KPI targets in 3 of 7 areas)**

## Detailed Performance Analysis

### Reactive Maintenance (Urgent Response)
Ventia has consistently missed the 4-hour urgent response target throughout the contract. Performance peaked at 93% in Year 2 and has declined to 87% in the most recent 12 months. Root causes identified:
- Insufficient after-hours trade staff (particularly plumbers and electricians)
- Subcontractor reliance for specialist trades with inconsistent availability
- Escalation process slow -- after-hours supervisor authorisation required before subcontractor deployment

This is the organisation's most significant service failure. Urgent reactive maintenance failures include 14 instances of air conditioning failure in occupied buildings lasting more than 8 hours, and 3 instances of security system failures overnight.

**Impact:** Service disruption, WHS concerns (heat events in occupied buildings), and unplanned capital expenditure when deferred repairs escalated.

### Fault Reporting and Documentation
The CAFM (Computer Aided Facilities Management) system data quality has been poor throughout the contract. Specific issues:
- 22% of completed work orders lack asset codes, making asset lifecycle analysis impossible
- Reactive jobs frequently closed without root cause documentation
- PPM records are not consistently linked to the asset register

This is a significant issue for the new contract given the organisation's asset management obligations.

## Relationship Assessment

The account management relationship has been professional. The dedicated account manager (Sarah Williams) is responsive and communicative. Senior management engagement from Ventia has been provided when requested. No disputes have been escalated to formal resolution. The organisation and Ventia have a functional working relationship.

---

## Key Lessons for New Contract

1. **Reactive maintenance response requires stronger contractual teeth.** The 4-hour target must be maintained but penalties for persistent underperformance (not just monthly at-risk) should be included.

2. **CAFM data quality must be a contractual requirement.** Minimum data completeness standards should be built into KPIs with specific deduction provisions.

3. **After-hours capability must be demonstrated at tender.** Tenderers should be required to demonstrate their after-hours staffing model, not just commit to response times.

4. **Subcontractor management must be explicit.** The new contract should require disclosure of all subcontractors and impose performance management obligations on them.

5. **Pricing model should reflect scope changes.** The schedule of rates for additional buildings should be clearly defined to avoid complex variation negotiations.

---

## Recommendation

Ventia should be permitted to tender for the new contract. Their performance, while below KPI targets in some areas, is within an acceptable range and they have significant institutional knowledge of the portfolio. However, the new contract should not be awarded without clear evidence that the reactive maintenance and documentation issues have been addressed in the tender response.
